The $78.5 Billion Blind Spot

Opioid complications are no longer just clinical errors; they are existential legal and financial threats.

$78.5B

Annual Economic Burden

Prescription-opioid misuse costs the U.S. an estimated $78.5B every year (Florence et al., Medical Care 2016) — while the conventional safety alerts meant to prevent harm are overridden 70–90% of the time (van der Sijs 2006; Phansalkar 2013, JAMIA).

46%

Undetected Risk

The PRODIGY Trial (Khanna et al., 2020) found 46% of general-care-floor patients receiving opioids had respiratory depression episodes on continuous monitoring — events that intermittent manual nursing checks routinely miss.

Litigation Risk

Corporate Negligence

Shift from malpractice to systemic liability. "Failure to monitor" claims increasingly name the institution, not just the clinician — an active, growing category of hospital liability exposure.

SMART on FHIR + CDS Hooks

How Integration Works

Three steps from clinician action to evidence-based recommendation — no additional training, no workflow disruption.

Step 1

Launch

Physician clicks a button in Epic or Cerner. The EMR opens a secure SMART on FHIR session — either embedded in the chart or in a new window.

Step 2

Handshake

OAuth 2.0 token exchange authenticates the session. The app queries patient demographics, lab values (eGFR, LFTs, QTc), and current medications via FHIR R4.

Step 3

Result

App loads instantly with patient context pre-filled. Safety gates auto-execute, OIRD risk is scored, and the clinician receives gated, evidence-based recommendations.

CDS Hooks
BPA Integration

Real-Time Intervention at Point of Prescribing

When a clinician selects or signs an opioid order, Epic fires a CDS Hook to our service endpoint. The Precision Analgesia engine evaluates all 10 safety rules in real-time and returns actionable cards — warnings, dose adjustments, and alternative recommendations.

For critical safety gates (renal contraindications, pediatric black box warnings), hospital Epic teams configure Best Practice Advisories (BPAs) powered by our logic — requiring documented clinical justification to override — configured and owned by the hospital's own Epic team.

Implementation Timeline
