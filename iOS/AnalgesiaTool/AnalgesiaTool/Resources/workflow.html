Reducing Cognitive Load in
High-Acuity Environments.

Calculation errors are often symptoms of interface fatigue. Opioid Precision streamlines the prescribing interface based on clinical context, suppressing non-urgent data while highlighting lethal parameters.

Trauma Bay Protocol

In resuscitation scenarios, "alert fatigue" can be fatal. The Trauma Bay Protocol activates a stripped-down interface designed for speed and safety.

  • Signal-to-Noise Optimization Hides non-urgent fields (e.g., insurance data, chronic taper history) to focus purely on acute stabilization.
  • Persistent Safety Rails Despite the simplified view, "Lethal Gates" (Renal function, Benzodiazepine co-load) remain permanently visible and active.
  • Conservative Defaults Auto-applies a 50% dose reduction for incomplete cross tolerance, requiring an active override to prescribe full calculated equivalence.

Context-Aware Formulary

Standard calculators present a "Wall of Zeros"—a list of 20+ opioids, most irrelevant to the patient. Opioid Precision filters the formulary based on the clinical setting.

The "Wall of Zeros" Problem

Legacy interfaces show Meperidine, Levorphanol, and Oxymorphone alongside standard agents, increasing the probability of selection error.

Active-Only Filtering

If "Trauma" is selected, the list narrows to Fentanyl, Hydromorphone, and Morphine. Oral agents and long-acting formulations are suppressed.

Contraindication Masks

Agents contraindicated by patient physiology (e.g., Codeine in pediatric patients or poor CYP2D6 metabolizers) are removed from the selection set entirely.

Codeine: BLOCKED (Age <18)
Documentation Engine

Resident Hero™

In litigious environments, defensibility is paramount. The system auto-generates a chart-ready risk assessment that details the rationale behind every dose and rotation.

Rather than simply documenting "Dose X given," the engine documents the absence of error: explicitly noting that renal function was checked, that interactions were screened, and that safety reductions were applied.
